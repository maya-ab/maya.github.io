Helping bees is an app that aims to connect individuals seeking volunteer opportunities with organizations in need of volunteers. I worked on the app along-side another developer. I focused on developing the back end for the log-in, sign-up, and search pages. This project is still a work in progress.

  • As a volunteer, you can search through volunteer posts, like posts to view them later, and select fields which you are interested in volunteering in.
  • As an organization, you can add volunteer posts and view your uploaded posts on your profile page.
  • This project was built using Flutter, Firebase, and Google Maps.

This project was created as part of a project for my data analytics course. We worked in teams but each had separate specific research topics. Here I'm displaying only my section of the research which focused on CO2 emissions and GDP. My research questions were:
  • How does the relationship between GDP and CO2 emissions compare for the 15 highest income Countries vs the 15 lowest income countries over recent years? What about during financial crises periods?
  • How does the average CO2 emissions per capita of high GDP countries compare to low GDP countries, and the rest of the world? Has this changed over the years?
All of the analysis was done in python. I utilized pandas, numpy, seaborn, and matlab libraries to create visualizations from my two datasets. Using these visualizations I answered my research questions (Only a portion of my visualizations are displayed here, the rest can be found on github). Click on an image to get a more up close view.

This video sharing website was made for my capstone course. Me and a team of 4 other computer science students have been working together in developing it. We utilized AWS, Amplify, react, and javascript to implement this website. I mainly worked on the front end of the record video and upload video pages, as well as the backend portion of the edit profile page. You can check it out yourself here: www.cosc499team20.com.

  • Users can log in and record videos via their browsers
  • These videos can then be uploaded, downloaded, or played back
  • Users can also simultaneously upload videos they had pre-recorded if they'd like
  • Uploaded videos get saved to an S3 bucket and can be watched back on the library page
  • Uploaded videos can be sent to other users registered with the website along with a description
  • More features to arrive...
